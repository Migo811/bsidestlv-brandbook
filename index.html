01 — Who We Are

Real research.
No shortcuts.

BSidesTLV was founded in 2013 as part of the global Security BSides movement — a network of community-organised security conferences that put real research ahead of commercial interests.

We bring together security researchers, engineers, students, hackers, and industry leaders for a full day of world-class talks, hands-on villages, and one of the most respected CTF competitions in the region.

We are a registered non-profit. Every shekel goes back to the community: better talks, more accessible registration, stronger CTF infrastructure, and a culture that welcomes everyone from first-time participants to veterans of the scene.

We never sell speaking slots, and we don't share participant data with sponsors. The price is never the story.

"Local energy. Global credibility."

Part of the global Security BSides network — a movement that gave communities the tools to run their own conferences without commercial compromise. Tel Aviv's chapter has become one of the most respected in the world.

02 — What We Stand For

The non-negotiables.

These aren't guidelines. They're the foundation. Everything we publish, every partnership we accept, every decision we make is measured against these.

01
No pay-to-speak. Ever.
Speaking at BSidesTLV is earned through the quality of research — not budget. No sponsor buys a keynote. The CFP process is blind. That's not a policy. That's a promise.
02
Participant data stays with participants.
Attendees choose to be here. We never hand their information to sponsors, partners, or anyone else. No lead lists. No data exports. No exceptions.
03
Every shekel goes back to the community.
We are a non-profit. Revenue funds the event — better infrastructure, accessible registration, a stronger CTF. There is no margin. There are no shareholders.
04
Research first. Always.
BSidesTLV exists for the talks that don't get accepted at polished commercial conferences. Too raw. Too niche. Too honest. That's exactly who we're here for.
05
Community over hype.
We don't chase trends. We don't optimise for impressions. If a decision serves the community, it's right. If it serves the brand first — reconsider it.
06
Volunteer work is credited work.
The people who build BSidesTLV give their time for free. That contribution is acknowledged, attributed, and not overridden.

04 — Voice & Tone

How BSidesTLV speaks.

Our audience is technically sophisticated and deeply allergic to marketing fluff. They can tell the difference between a real community and a brand performing community. Write accordingly.

We sound like this
+ Direct. We say what we mean without filler.
+ Dry and confident. Wit comes from honesty, not cleverness.
+ Earned. We reference the community's real work.
+ Inclusive without being precious. First-timers welcome. Veterans home.
+ Specific. Real formats, real durations, real stakes.
+ Occasionally dark. This is a hacker conference. Own it.
We don't sound like this
— Corporate enthusiasm. "We're thrilled to announce…"
— AI-optimised buzzwords. "Unlock your potential."
— Generic event copy. "Network with industry leaders."
— Over-produced warmth. "Join our amazing community!"
— Hype without substance. Claims we can't stand behind.
— Manufactured urgency for its own sake.
Wrong

"Join us for an exciting journey into the future of cybersecurity at BSidesTLV 2026, where the brightest minds come together to shape tomorrow's threat landscape!"

Right

"BSidesTLV is built on the talks that don't get accepted anywhere else — the research that's too raw, too niche, or too honest for a polished conference slot. If you have something real to say, this is your table."

07 — For Speakers

You have something to say.

BSidesTLV is built on the talks that don't get accepted anywhere else. The CFP is fair, blind, and community-reviewed. Every voice belongs at this table — including yours.

// Process

Blind Review

Reviewers do not see your name, employer, or affiliation during initial scoring. We evaluate on originality, technical depth, community relevance, and clarity. Job title is not a criterion.

// Policy

No Pay-to-Speak

Speaking slots are never for sale. Sponsors do not get speaking access. A title-sponsor acknowledgment at opening is not a talk. The CFP process is separate from all commercial arrangements.

// Commitment

Everyone Hears Back

Accepted or not — we notify every submitter. If your talk isn't selected this year, the table is open again next year.

// Wanted

What We're Looking For

Offensive and defensive research. AI/ML security. Hardware hacking. Cloud and supply chain. Bug bounty. Tool releases. Policy. The human side of security. Anything that makes the room smarter.

// Not Wanted

What We Don't Accept

Product pitches. Vendor promotions. Talks already presented at BSidesTLV within two years. Anything that violates our Code of Conduct.

// Formats

Session Formats

20-minute talk. 40-minute talk. Workshop (hands-on, requires equipment list + cap). Village proposals submitted separately.

08 — For Sponsors

A seat at the
Mad Hatter's table.

Our audience is technically sophisticated, highly engaged, and deeply allergic to marketing fluff. Sponsoring BSidesTLV means putting your brand in a room where it has to earn its place. The organisations that do it well become part of the community.

We are a non-profit. Your investment goes directly into making the event better.

What sponsoring gets you
+Logo on website, stage backdrop, lanyard, and event programme.
+Complimentary participant registrations (tier dependent).
+Social media mentions — pre-event and day-of.
+Village or workshop co-branding at higher tiers.
+Access to a room full of people who are genuinely hard to reach any other way.
What it doesn't get you
×Participant data. No lead lists. No contact exports. Ever.
×Access to the CFP process or influence over speaker selection.
×A speaking slot. An acknowledgment is not a talk.
×The right to reject talks based on competitive interest.
×Logo placement implying editorial endorsement.

"If that's the room you want to be in — one where your brand has to earn its place — we'd love to have you at the table."
